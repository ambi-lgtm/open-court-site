At LocalGlobe, we've always believed in the power of networks — not in the transactional, hollow LinkedIn-connections kind of way, but in the deeply human sense of finding your people. The ones who challenge your ideas, cheer your wins, and show up when things are messy, not just during victory laps.

Origin

From Open Coffee
to Open Court

Open Court is a continuation of a legacy. Over 20 years ago, LocalGlobe co-founder Saul Klein helped launch The Open Coffee Club — a humble, community-first meetup in London that brought together founders and operators over morning espressos to swap notes on building startups.

That small experiment grew into a global grassroots movement, inspiring hundreds of similar gatherings around the world and supporting the early journeys of a host of now-iconic startups.

Open Court takes the spirit of Open Coffee — the openness, the informality, the peer-to-peer energy — and reimagines it for a new era of builders.

If Open Coffee was about getting ideas off the ground, Open Court is about propelling them forward — through connection, collaboration, and collective momentum.

Inspiration

Why club
culture?

To understand what we're going for, it helps to look sideways — not at other tech events, but at something perhaps less obvious: underground club culture.

Long before "community" became a VC buzzword, club culture had already cracked the code. The best underground nights weren't about hype — they were about belonging. Spaces that were diverse by default, not by design. The only common thread was the shared electricity of a room full of people who got it.

King's Cross's own legendary 90s venue, Bagley's, exemplified this perfectly — a magnet for misfits, makers and mavericks. Long before "ecosystem" became startup vernacular, Bagley's was already acting as an incubator.

Bagley's hosted secret gigs from Prince, Grace Jones, and Depeche Mode. Runway shows from Alexander McQueen. It was a seminal space for the UK Garage scene — a genre born from the cross-pollination of London's immigrant communities. A diverse, brilliant community of makers, dreamers and innovators, united by their openness, energy, and willingness to co-create.

Bagley's may be long gone, but its limitlessly innovative energy can still be felt as part of King's Cross's DNA. That spirit is what we're channelling at Open Court — reimagined for today's founder community.
